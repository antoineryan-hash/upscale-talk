IF YOUR MAC BLOCKED THE INSTALLER
("Apple could not verify 'Install upscale-talk.command' is free of malware")

That warning is normal and nothing is wrong with your Mac. The installer
is a plain script, not an App Store app, so your Mac has no way to check
who wrote it and plays it safe. Do NOT click "Move to Bin".

Pick ONE of the two fixes below.


FIX 1 - the Settings way (no typing, about 30 seconds)

  1. Double-click "Install upscale-talk.command". When the warning pops
     up, click "Done" (NOT "Move to Bin").
  2. Open System Settings, then click "Privacy & Security" in the sidebar.
  3. Scroll to the bottom. You will see a line saying
     "Install upscale-talk.command" was blocked.
  4. Click "Open Anyway" next to it, and put in your Mac password.
  5. Double-click the installer again. This time it opens and the
     install runs in a Terminal window. Follow the prompts from there.

  On older Macs (macOS 14 Sonoma or earlier) there is a shortcut:
  right-click the installer, choose "Open", then click "Open" in the
  popup. On newer Macs that shortcut no longer exists - use the steps
  above.


FIX 2 - the Terminal way (works on every Mac, about 20 seconds)

  1. Press Cmd + Space, type: Terminal   and press Enter.
  2. In the Terminal window type the word   bash   followed by a
     single space. Do not press Enter yet.
  3. Drag "Install upscale-talk.command" from this folder into the
     Terminal window. The file's location fills itself in.
  4. Press Enter. The installer starts right there in that window.


WHY DOES THIS HAPPEN?

Apple charges $99 a year for the developer certificate that makes this
warning go away. upscale-talk is a free tool built for the UpScale team,
so we skipped the certificate and kept it free.

If you want to check the installer for yourself: right-click
"Install upscale-talk.command", choose Open With > TextEdit. The whole
thing is readable text - you can see exactly what it does before you
run it.
